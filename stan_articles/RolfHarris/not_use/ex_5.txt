Inside the trial and the dark side of Rolf Harris | The Times
The long trial of the veteran entertainer revealed the true nature of his abusive behaviour. Yet it was also a theatre of the absurd
The trial of Rolf Harris was always going to be a time warp and Southwark crown court, with its withered Formica furniture and its creaking lifts, made the perfect setting. It was built in 1983, the year Harris wrote his jaunty bestselling autobiography, Can You Tell What it is Yet?. It was also the year he first started abusing a then 13-year-old girl. Could he have ever expected to end up here?
