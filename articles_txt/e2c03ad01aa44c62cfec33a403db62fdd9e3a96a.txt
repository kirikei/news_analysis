DENVER (AP) — One of the authors of Colorado's Taxpayer's Bill of Rights has been ordered to stand trial for possible parole violations.
Douglas Bruce appeared in court Friday and said authorities are picking on him. Bruce said he has been a model probationer for three years and it's all a misunderstanding.
Bruce was convicted in 2012 for tax evasion, filing a false tax return and trying to influence a public servant. He served 104 days for the felony convictions.
