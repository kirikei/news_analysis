People protest in Miami after the district court judge in Brownsville, Texas, issued a preliminary injunction that temporarily blocks President Obama's immigration orders.
Cities and counties across the country will join Mayor de Blasio to stand up for President Obama’s immigration executive orders in court.
The group will file a friend of the court brief in a lawsuit challenging Obama’s move to allow 5 million undocumented immigrants to avoid deportation.
In response to a lawsuit filed by Texas and other states, a judge temporarily blocked Obama’s orders from taking effect.
Andrew Burton/Getty Images
Mayor de Blasio and other city and county leaders will file a friend of the court brief in support of Obama's plan.
De Blasio, Los Angeles Mayor Eric Garcetti, and other local leaders — who together represent 43 million people — argue that the decision hurts city tax coffers and thwarts local law enforcement efforts.
“Continuing to delay implementation of the President’s executive action on immigration hurts our economy and puts families at risk,” de Blasio said.
