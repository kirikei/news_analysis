Indictments in the federal investigation into lane closings on the George Washington Bridge could be announced as early as next week, it was reported Wednesday.
In recent weeks, federal investigators have interviewed members of the Borough Council in Fort Lee, N.J., the town gridlocked when traffic came to a standstill for several days in September 2013, the New York Times reported.
The headache-inducing gridlock was revealed to be the result of orders from allies of Gov. Chris Christie after the mayor of Fort Lee declined to endorse Christie’s reelection bid.
