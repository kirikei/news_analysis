Published April 11, 2015
Police were investigating the shooting as a suicide. Officials reported the shooter was "neutralized" and the lockdown was considered precautionary. Police also were investigating a suspicious package in connection with the shooting.

Fox News confirmed the shooter died of ‎a self-inflicted gunshot wound. Police said they received reports of gunfire just before 2 p.m.
A senior law enforcement official told  Fox News the FBI's Joint Terrorism Task Force was on the scene providing "bomb tech" support. 
The source said there was no indication of terrorism but it is an active crime scene.
The lockdown comes during Washington's annual Cherry Blossom Festival, which attracts scores of tourists to Capitol Hill and the downtown Mall area.
Multiple law enforcement and emergency services responded quickly, including DC Metro Police Department and the DC Fire Department. 
MyFoxDC reported initially that the suspected gunman was "disarmed and deceased."
"U.S. Capitol Police are locking down the U.S. Capitol Building and the Capitol Visitor Center due to a potential security threat," Capitol Police spokeswoman Kimberly Schneider said. She said no one was allowed to enter or exit the two buildings.
Fox News reported the suspicious package was the dead man's bag, found on the lower west terrace of the building.
Police closed roads in the vicinity of the area.
Congress has been on spring break for two weeks and lawmakers are set to return to work Monday.
The Associated Press contributed to this report.
